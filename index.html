Dan Sullivan (R)

Incumbent US Senator

Background

Two-term senator seeking a third term; running on his committee seniority and national security credentials, while his campaign has been complicated by a voter-confusion controversy involving a second candidate with the same name. (source)

Current storyline

The Sullivan race has been dominated by a controversy over a same-named challenger, Dan J. Sullivan of Petersburg, whose late-filed candidacy Republicans and the incumbent's campaign allege was designed to confuse voters and siphon support to help Democrat Mary Peltola. Alaska's Division of Elections initially disqualified the challenger, but courts—first a Superior Court judge and then the Alaska Supreme Court—ordered him reinstated on the primary ballot, while the NRSC filed an FEC complaint alleging coordination between the challenger and a Democratic consultant. NBC News has since reported that the FBI, the Alaska attorney general's office, and the U.S. attorney's office are investigating whether the challenger's campaign was part of a conspiracy to confuse voters, though Peltola's campaign has denied any involvement. The episode has unfolded alongside a broader, closely watched contest between Sullivan and Peltola that both parties view as pivotal to Senate control in 2026.

Mary Peltola (D)

Background

Former U.S. Representative and the first Alaska Native elected to Congress, narrowly unseated in 2024; running an "affordability" campaign focused on the cost of living and rural/fisheries issues as one of Democrats' top recruits nationally. (source)

Current storyline

The race has been defined by its unusual competitiveness for a heavily Republican-leaning state, with polling showing the contest within a few points throughout the year. A late-June/early-July New York Times/Siena poll found Sullivan leading Peltola 47%-45% among likely voters, a result that contradicted Peltola campaign messaging and differed from Alaska Survey Research polls showing Peltola with a roughly five-point lead. The race has also been shaped by Sen. Lisa Murkowski's decision to back Sullivan despite having endorsed Peltola in the past and describing her as a long-time friend, saying she believes keeping a Republican Senate majority is in Alaska's best interest. Additional coverage has focused on a separate ballot controversy involving a like-named Republican challenger, Dan J. Sullivan, whom Sen. Sullivan's campaign called a 'sham candidate' after he was initially removed from and then restored to the ballot by courts.

Susan Collins (R)

Incumbent US Senator

Background

Five-term senator and chair of the Senate Appropriations Committee; running on her seniority and a record of delivering federal funding for Maine rather than her decades in Washington. (source)

Current storyline

The defining story in the race has shifted from Collins herself to the turmoil in the Democratic field: nominee Graham Platner, who won the June 9 primary after Gov. Janet Mills suspended her campaign, is facing a new sexual assault allegation that he denies, prompting numerous Democratic leaders and groups, including VoteVets and the Working Families Party, to rescind endorsements and call on him to withdraw. Collins has largely stayed above the fray while touting her seniority and Appropriations Committee chairmanship, and has received supportive comments from President Trump, who has said he would campaign for her. Coverage from the Washington Post, Axios, CNBC, and the Associated Press has focused heavily on whether Platner will remain in the race and who might replace him if he exits, given Maine's status as one of the most competitive Senate contests of the cycle.

Under Maine law Platner would need to withdraw by July 13 for the state Democratic Party to name a replacement nominee by July 27, and press coverage has focused on potential replacement candidates such as Troy Jackson and Shenna Bellows.

Ohio Senate

General election 2026-11-03

Cook: Toss Up Crystal Ball: Toss-up
JH

Jon Husted (R)

Incumbent US Senator (appointed)

Background

Appointed to the Senate in January 2025 after serving as Ohio's lieutenant governor and secretary of state; running with Trump's endorsement on his record of economic development and job-training initiatives. (source)

Current storyline

Husted, running to keep the seat he was appointed to, faces Democratic former Senator Sherrod Brown in a race widely rated a toss-up despite Ohio's recent Republican lean. Husted secured President Trump's early endorsement and has emphasized ties to Trump in his campaign advertising, while also touting several labor union endorsements he flipped from Brown's 2024 coalition. Public polling has been volatile and conflicting, with some surveys (Fox News, AARP) showing Brown ahead by several points and others (New York Times/Siena, Emerson) showing Husted with a narrow lead, underscoring how competitive the contest has become. In March 2026, Husted also testified for the defense in the FirstEnergy bribery scandal criminal trial, describing a 2018 dinner meeting with then governor-elect DeWine and FirstEnergy executives later indicted in the $60 million bribery scheme.

Sherrod Brown (D)

Former US Senator

Background

Three-term former senator attempting a comeback after his 2024 defeat; running on affordability, health care costs, and his long-standing 'fighting for workers' brand that helped him win statewide in a red-trending state for decades. (source)

Current storyline

Brown, attempting a political comeback after his 2024 loss, is running against Republican incumbent Jon Husted (appointed to the seat after JD Vance became vice president) in a race both parties see as pivotal to Senate control. The campaign has centered on affordability and health care, with Brown attacking Husted over ACA subsidy votes and rising prices while Husted blames Brown's 32-year Washington record for current problems. In June 2026, both campaigns ran dueling ads accusing each other's donors of ties to Jeffrey Epstein associates, a story fact-checked by PolitiFact. Brown has maintained a fundraising advantage, reporting far more cash on hand than Husted, even as the two campaigns and outside groups are expected to spend record sums in what is being called one of the most expensive Senate races in the country.
