Dan Sullivan (R)

Incumbent US Senator

Background

Two-term senator seeking a third term; running on his committee seniority and national security credentials, while his campaign has been complicated by a voter-confusion controversy involving a second candidate with the same name. (source)

Current storyline

The Sullivan race has been dominated by a controversy over a same-named challenger, Dan J. Sullivan of Petersburg, whose late-filed candidacy Republicans and the incumbent's campaign allege was designed to confuse voters and siphon support to help Democrat Mary Peltola. Alaska's Division of Elections initially disqualified the challenger, but courts—first a Superior Court judge and then the Alaska Supreme Court—ordered him reinstated on the primary ballot, while the NRSC filed an FEC complaint alleging coordination between the challenger and a Democratic consultant. NBC News has since reported that the FBI, the Alaska attorney general's office, and the U.S. attorney's office are investigating whether the challenger's campaign was part of a conspiracy to confuse voters, though Peltola's campaign has denied any involvement. The episode has unfolded alongside a broader, closely watched contest between Sullivan and Peltola that both parties view as pivotal to Senate control in 2026.
